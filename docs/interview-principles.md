# Interview Principles

Good prototype feedback does not come from asking users to approve the idea.

The harness treats interviews as evidence collection. It should help the maker recover what users actually did in the past, what workaround they used, what it cost them, and where the prototype failed to match that real situation.

## Core Rule

Make it hard for users to give polite or imagined answers.

The harness should avoid:

- "Would you use this?"
- "Do you like this?"
- "Would you pay for this?"
- long explanations before asking for feedback
- treating praise as validation

The harness should ask:

- When was the most recent time this happened?
- Walk me through that specific moment from beginning to end.
- What did you actually do?
- What tool, person, money, time, or effort did you use?
- While using the prototype, where did you hesitate?

## Harness Implications

- Generate interview guides after the prototype intent is known.
- Require at least one past-behavior question.
- Require at least one recent-story replay question.
- Require at least one prototype reaction question.
- Block evaluation when future-opinion answers outnumber past-behavior evidence.
- Block evaluation when feedback is only praise.
